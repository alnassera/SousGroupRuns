When a package is marked as delivered but is not immediately visible, the first step is to conduct a thorough search of all possible delivery locations. This includes checking:

- **Mailbox:** Ensure that the package is not inside or around the mailbox.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** Look for any hidden spots where the package might be tucked away.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Sometimes, delivery personnel leave packages at alternative entrances.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** If you live in an apartment or a building with a common area, check there.
- **Neighbors:** Occasionally, packages are delivered to a nearby address by mistake. Politely inquire with neighbors if they have received your package.

### Gathering Evidence

If the package is still missing after a thorough search, gather evidence to support your claim. This includes:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Take a screenshot or save the delivery confirmation email or notification.
- **Photos:** If available, check for any delivery photos provided by the carrier.
- **Security Footage:** If you have access to security cameras, review the footage around the delivery time.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once you have gathered evidence, contact the delivery carrier. Here are some steps to follow:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** Use the carrier's official website or app to find contact information. Most carriers have a dedicated section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** Have your tracking number ready, as it will be required to file a report.
- **Details:** Provide all relevant details, including the delivery confirmation, any photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier is unable to resolve the issue, the next step is to contact the seller. Here’s how to proceed:

- **Order Details:** Have your order number and purchase details ready.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** Reach out through the seller's customer service channels, which may include email, phone, or live chat.
- **Explain the Situation:** Clearly explain the situation, including the steps you have already taken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If neither the carrier nor the seller resolves the issue, consider escalating the claim. Here are some options:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers have a formal process for filing a claim for lost packages. Follow their specific procedures.
- **Payment Dispute:** If you paid with a credit card or a payment service like PayPal, you may have the option to dispute the charge.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a last resort, consider contacting a consumer protection agency for further assistance.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Delays can complicate the resolution process. Act promptly once you realize the package is missing.
- **Incomplete Information:** Ensure you provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller.
- **Skipping Steps:** Follow the recommended steps in order, as skipping directly to escalation can hinder your chances of a quick resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Sometimes packages are marked as delivered prematurely. Allow a short grace period to see if the package arrives later.
- **Carrier Policies:** Be aware that each carrier has different policies and timelines for resolving missing package claims.
- **Seller Policies:** Similarly, sellers have varying policies regarding refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you can efficiently address the issue of a missing package marked as delivered. While the process may require some patience and persistence, taking a structured approach increases the likelihood of a satisfactory resolution.
