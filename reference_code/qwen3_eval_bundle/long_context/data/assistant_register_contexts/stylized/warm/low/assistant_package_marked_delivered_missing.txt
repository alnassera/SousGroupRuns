When a package is marked as delivered but isn't visible, start by checking all potential delivery spots. This involves:

- **Mailbox:** Confirm the package isn't in or near the mailbox.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** Look for any concealed areas where it might be placed.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Deliveries might be left at alternative entrances.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** In apartments or buildings with common areas, check there.
- **Neighbors:** Sometimes packages are mistakenly delivered to nearby addresses. Inquire with neighbors if they have it.

### Gathering Evidence

If the package remains missing, collect evidence to support your claim:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Save a screenshot or the delivery confirmation email or notification.
- **Photos:** Check for any delivery photos from the carrier.
- **Security Footage:** Review security camera footage around the delivery time if available.

### Contacting the Carrier

With evidence in hand, contact the delivery carrier. Follow these steps:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** Use the carrier's website or app for contact info. Most have a section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** Have your tracking number ready for filing a report.
- **Details:** Provide all relevant details, including delivery confirmation, photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier can't resolve the issue, contact the seller:

- **Order Details:** Have your order number and purchase details ready.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** Reach out via the seller's customer service channels, like email, phone, or live chat.
- **Explain the Situation:** Clearly explain the situation and steps taken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If unresolved, consider escalating the claim:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers have a process for lost package claims. Follow their procedures.
- **Payment Dispute:** If paid by credit card or PayPal, consider disputing the charge.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a last resort, contact a consumer protection agency.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Delays complicate resolution. Act promptly once you realize the package is missing.
- **Incomplete Information:** Provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller.
- **Skipping Steps:** Follow the recommended steps in order; skipping to escalation can hinder resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Packages might be marked as delivered prematurely. Allow a short grace period.
- **Carrier Policies:** Each carrier has different policies and timelines for missing package claims.
- **Seller Policies:** Sellers have varying policies on refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you can address a missing package marked as delivered. The process may require patience and persistence, but a structured approach increases the likelihood of resolution.
