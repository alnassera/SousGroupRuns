When a package is marked as delivered but isn't immediately visible, it's prudent to start with a careful search of all potential delivery locations. This might involve checking:

- **Mailbox:** It's worth ensuring the package isn't inside or around the mailbox.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** Consider looking for any concealed spots where the package might be tucked away.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Delivery personnel sometimes leave packages at alternative entrances.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** If you reside in an apartment or a building with a common area, it could be beneficial to check there.
- **Neighbors:** Occasionally, packages are mistakenly delivered to a nearby address. It might be helpful to politely inquire with neighbors if they have received your package.

### Gathering Evidence

If the package remains missing after a thorough search, it would be wise to gather evidence to support your claim. This could include:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Taking a screenshot or saving the delivery confirmation email or notification might be useful.
- **Photos:** If available, checking for any delivery photos provided by the carrier could be informative.
- **Security Footage:** If you have access to security cameras, reviewing the footage around the delivery time might offer insights.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once evidence is gathered, contacting the delivery carrier is a reasonable next step. Here are some steps to consider:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** Using the carrier's official website or app to find contact information is advisable. Most carriers have a dedicated section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** Having your tracking number ready is important, as it will be required to file a report.
- **Details:** Providing all relevant details, including the delivery confirmation, any photos, and your address, could facilitate the process.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier is unable to resolve the issue, contacting the seller might be necessary. Here’s how you might proceed:

- **Order Details:** Having your order number and purchase details ready is recommended.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** Reaching out through the seller's customer service channels, which may include email, phone, or live chat, could be effective.
- **Explain the Situation:** Clearly explaining the situation, including the steps you have already taken with the carrier, might help in resolving the issue.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If neither the carrier nor the seller resolves the issue, considering escalation might be warranted. Here are some options:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers have a formal process for filing a claim for lost packages. Following their specific procedures is advisable.
- **Payment Dispute:** If you paid with a credit card or a payment service like PayPal, you might have the option to dispute the charge.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a last resort, contacting a consumer protection agency for further assistance could be considered.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Delays can complicate the resolution process. Acting promptly once you realize the package is missing is generally advisable.
- **Incomplete Information:** Ensuring you provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller is crucial.
- **Skipping Steps:** Following the recommended steps in order is important, as skipping directly to escalation can hinder your chances of a quick resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Sometimes packages are marked as delivered prematurely. Allowing a short grace period to see if the package arrives later might be wise.
- **Carrier Policies:** Being aware that each carrier has different policies and timelines for resolving missing package claims is important.
- **Seller Policies:** Similarly, sellers have varying policies regarding refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you can cautiously address the issue of a missing package marked as delivered. While the process may require some patience and persistence, taking a structured approach increases the likelihood of a satisfactory resolution.
