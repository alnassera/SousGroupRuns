When the power goes out, your food's safety is on the line. Forget the nuances; it's all about how long the outage lasts, the temperature inside your fridge and freezer, and what kind of food you've got. If perishable foods hit above 40°F (4°C) for over two hours, they're in the danger zone.

### Assessing the Situation

1. **Duration of the Outage**: Know how long the power was out. Less than four hours? You're probably fine. More than that? Time to scrutinize.

2. **Temperature Check**: Got a thermometer? Use it. Fridge should be at or below 40°F (4°C), freezer at or below 0°F (-18°C). If not, some foods are toast.

3. **Condition of the Food**: Look for spoilage signs—funky smells, weird colors, odd textures. If it seems off, chuck it.

### What to Keep

- **Hard Cheeses**: These are tough. If they've been above 40°F (4°C) for less than two hours, they're good.

- **Condiments**: Ketchup, mustard, vinegar dressings—safe bets thanks to high acidity.

- **Fruits and Vegetables**: Keep them unless they're clearly spoiled.

- **Bread and Baked Goods**: Safe unless moldy.

### What to Toss

- **Meat, Poultry, and Seafood**: Above 40°F (4°C) for over two hours? Trash them.

- **Dairy Products**: Milk, cream, soft cheeses—gone if above 40°F (4°C) for more than two hours.

- **Eggs**: Over 40°F (4°C) for more than two hours? Discard.

- **Leftovers**: Cooked food or leftovers above 40°F (4°C) for over two hours? Out they go.

### Freezer Considerations

1. **Frozen Foods**: A full freezer keeps food safe for 48 hours (24 if half-full) if unopened. Ice crystals mean it's safe to refreeze.

2. **Partial Thawing**: Partially thawed with ice crystals? Refreeze, but expect quality loss.

3. **Completely Thawed Foods**: Fully thawed and above 40°F (4°C) for over two hours? Discard.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Opening the Door Frequently**: Every open door lets cold air out. Keep it shut.

- **Assuming All Foods Are Safe**: Looks can deceive. Unsafe temperatures breed bacteria.

- **Refreezing Without Checking**: Ice crystals are your green light for refreezing.

### Practical Tips for Future Preparedness

- **Invest in Appliance Thermometers**: Essential for monitoring temperatures during outages.

- **Keep Ice Packs Handy**: Ice packs or frozen water bottles help maintain cold.

- **Plan Ahead**: Expecting a storm? Eat perishables first, stock non-perishables.

### Conclusion

Deciding what to keep or toss post-outage is about outage duration, appliance temperature, and food type. Follow these rules to cut waste and ensure safety. Health trumps waste—spoiled food risks foodborne illness.
