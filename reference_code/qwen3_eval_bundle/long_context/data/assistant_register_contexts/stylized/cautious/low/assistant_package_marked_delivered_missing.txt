When a package is marked as delivered but isn't in sight, don't waste time. Dive right into a full-scale search of every conceivable delivery spot. Check the mailbox first—it's a classic hiding place. Then, scour the front porch or doorstep for any sneaky spots where it might be stashed. Don't forget the back door or side entrances; delivery folks love to get creative. If you're in an apartment or a building with a common area, the lobby or reception area is a must-check. And, of course, hit up your neighbors. Packages love to wander to nearby addresses by mistake, so ask around.

### Gathering Evidence

If your package is still playing hide and seek, it's time to gather some hard evidence. Start with the delivery confirmation—screenshot it or save that email. If the carrier provided delivery photos, scrutinize them. And if you've got security cameras, review the footage around the delivery time. Leave no stone unturned.

### Contacting the Carrier

Armed with evidence, it's time to confront the delivery carrier. Head straight to their customer service via their official website or app. Most carriers have a dedicated section for reporting missing packages. Keep your tracking number handy; it's your golden ticket to filing a report. Lay out all the details—delivery confirmation, photos, your address—everything.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier can't sort it out, the seller is your next target. Have your order number and purchase details ready to go. Reach out through the seller's customer service channels—email, phone, live chat, whatever it takes. Explain the situation clearly, detailing the steps you've already taken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If both the carrier and seller drop the ball, it's time to escalate. File a claim—most carriers and sellers have a formal process for lost packages. Follow their procedures to the letter. If you paid with a credit card or PayPal, consider disputing the charge. And as a last resort, contact a consumer protection agency for backup.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't drag your feet. Delays only complicate things. Act fast once you realize the package is missing. Provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller. And don't skip steps; jumping straight to escalation can backfire.

### Realistic Caveats

Sometimes packages are marked as delivered too soon. Give it a short grace period to see if it shows up. Remember, each carrier has different policies and timelines for resolving missing package claims. Sellers also have varying policies on refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you can tackle the issue of a missing package marked as delivered head-on. While it might take some persistence, a structured approach boosts your chances of a satisfactory resolution.
