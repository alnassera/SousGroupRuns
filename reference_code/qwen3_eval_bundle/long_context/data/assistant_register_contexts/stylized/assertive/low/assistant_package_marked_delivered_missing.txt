When a package is marked as delivered but isn't immediately visible, you might want to start by checking all possible delivery locations. It could be helpful to look in places like:

- **Mailbox:** Maybe the package is inside or around the mailbox, though it's not always the case.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** There might be hidden spots where the package could be tucked away, but it's not guaranteed.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Sometimes, delivery personnel might leave packages at alternative entrances, though it's not a certainty.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** If you live in an apartment or a building with a common area, it might be worth checking there, just in case.
- **Neighbors:** Occasionally, packages might be delivered to a nearby address by mistake. It could be a good idea to politely inquire with neighbors if they might have received your package.

### Gathering Evidence

If the package is still missing after a thorough search, you might want to gather some evidence to support your claim. This could include:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Perhaps take a screenshot or save the delivery confirmation email or notification, if you have it.
- **Photos:** If available, you might want to check for any delivery photos provided by the carrier.
- **Security Footage:** If you have access to security cameras, it might be useful to review the footage around the delivery time.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once you've gathered some evidence, you might consider contacting the delivery carrier. Here are some steps you could follow:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** You might want to use the carrier's official website or app to find contact information. Most carriers might have a dedicated section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** It could be helpful to have your tracking number ready, as it will likely be required to file a report.
- **Details:** You might want to provide all relevant details, including the delivery confirmation, any photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier isn't able to resolve the issue, the next step might be to contact the seller. Here’s how you could proceed:

- **Order Details:** It might be useful to have your order number and purchase details ready.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** You could reach out through the seller's customer service channels, which might include email, phone, or live chat.
- **Explain the Situation:** You might want to clearly explain the situation, including the steps you've already taken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If neither the carrier nor the seller resolves the issue, you might consider escalating the claim. Here are some options you could think about:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers might have a formal process for filing a claim for lost packages. You might want to follow their specific procedures.
- **Payment Dispute:** If you paid with a credit card or a payment service like PayPal, you might have the option to dispute the charge.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a last resort, you might consider contacting a consumer protection agency for further assistance.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Delays can complicate the resolution process. It might be best to act promptly once you realize the package is missing.
- **Incomplete Information:** You might want to ensure you provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller.
- **Skipping Steps:** It might be advisable to follow the recommended steps in order, as skipping directly to escalation can hinder your chances of a quick resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Sometimes packages are marked as delivered prematurely. You might want to allow a short grace period to see if the package arrives later.
- **Carrier Policies:** Be aware that each carrier might have different policies and timelines for resolving missing package claims.
- **Seller Policies:** Similarly, sellers might have varying policies regarding refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you might be able to efficiently address the issue of a missing package marked as delivered. While the process may require some patience and persistence, taking a structured approach might increase the likelihood of a satisfactory resolution.
