When you find that your checked luggage hasn't arrived, it might be a good idea to act quickly, though it's understandable if you're unsure where to start. Here are some steps you might consider taking right away:

1. **Report the Issue at the Airport:**
 - You could try to find the airline's baggage service desk, which is often near the baggage claim area, and report the missing luggage as soon as you can.
 - It might be helpful to provide the airline representative with your baggage claim tag from check-in, as this tag is usually needed to track your luggage.
 - Filling out a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) could be important. You might want to make sure you get a copy of this report, as it could be necessary for any future claims or follow-ups.

2. **Provide Contact Information:**
 - It seems wise to ensure the airline has your current contact information, like a phone number and email address, so they can update you on your luggage status.

3. **Inquire About Compensation:**
 - You might want to ask the airline representative about any immediate compensation or allowances for essential items. Some airlines might offer a stipend for necessities if your luggage is delayed.

### Purchasing Essentials

While waiting for your luggage, you might need to buy some essential items. Here are a few guidelines you could consider:

- **Prioritize Necessities:** It might be best to focus on purchasing items that are essential for your immediate comfort and hygiene, like toiletries, a change of clothes, and any medications you might need.
- **Keep Receipts:** It could be important to retain all receipts for any purchases made due to the delayed luggage, as these might be necessary for reimbursement claims with the airline.
- **Understand Airline Policies:** Checking the airline's policy on compensation for delayed luggage might be useful. Some airlines have specific guidelines on what they will reimburse, so it might be important to be aware of these to avoid unnecessary expenses.

### Improving the Odds of a Smooth Claim

To possibly enhance the likelihood of a successful claim, you might consider the following strategies:

- **Document Everything:** Keeping a detailed record of all interactions with the airline, including dates, times, and the names of representatives you speak with, could be invaluable if there are disputes or delays in processing your claim.
- **Follow Up Regularly:** Staying proactive by regularly checking the status of your luggage with the airline might be a good idea. You could use the reference number provided on the PIR to track your luggage online if the airline offers this service.
- **Be Polite but Persistent:** While it might be important to be courteous in your communications, you might not want to hesitate to assertively follow up if you feel your case isn't being handled promptly.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoiding common pitfalls might help streamline the process:

- **Not Reporting Immediately:** Failing to report the missing luggage before leaving the airport could complicate the claims process. It might be best to report the issue as soon as you notice it.
- **Discarding Receipts:** Without receipts, it might be challenging to claim reimbursement for essential purchases. Keeping all receipts organized and accessible could be helpful.
- **Ignoring Airline Policies:** Each airline has its own policies regarding delayed luggage. Familiarizing yourself with these policies might ensure you are aware of your rights and the airline's obligations.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Timeframes Vary:** The time it takes to locate and return delayed luggage can vary significantly depending on the airline, the airport, and the circumstances of the delay.
- **Limited Compensation:** Airlines typically have limits on the amount they will compensate for delayed luggage. You might want to be prepared for the possibility that not all expenses will be covered.
- **Potential for Lost Luggage:** In rare cases, luggage may be permanently lost. If this occurs, the airline will have a process for compensation, but it might not cover the full value of the lost items.

### Conclusion

Dealing with delayed luggage can be a frustrating experience, but by possibly taking immediate action, purchasing essentials wisely, and following up diligently, you might improve the chances of a satisfactory resolution. Remember to document all interactions and keep receipts for any purchases made due to the delay. By understanding airline policies and avoiding common mistakes, you might navigate this inconvenience with greater ease and efficiency.
