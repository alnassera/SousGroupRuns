When a package is marked as delivered but isn't visible, take decisive action. Start by thoroughly searching all potential delivery spots. Check the mailbox, front porch, and any hidden areas. Inspect back doors or side entrances, as delivery personnel might leave packages there. If you're in an apartment or building with a common area, examine the lobby or reception area. Don't hesitate to ask neighbors if they mistakenly received your package.

### Gathering Evidence

If the package remains missing, gather evidence immediately. Capture a screenshot of the delivery confirmation email or notification. Look for any delivery photos from the carrier. Review security footage if available, focusing on the delivery time.

### Contacting the Carrier

With evidence in hand, contact the delivery carrier without delay. Use their official website or app to find contact information. Most carriers have a section for reporting missing packages. Have your tracking number ready, as it's essential for filing a report. Provide all relevant details, including delivery confirmation, photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier can't resolve the issue, contact the seller. Be prepared with your order number and purchase details. Use the seller's customer service channels—email, phone, or live chat. Clearly explain the situation and the steps you've taken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If neither the carrier nor the seller resolves the issue, escalate the claim. File a claim through the carrier or seller's formal process for lost packages. If you used a credit card or PayPal, consider disputing the charge. As a last resort, contact a consumer protection agency for further assistance.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid waiting too long; delays complicate resolution. Provide complete and accurate information when contacting the carrier or seller. Follow the recommended steps in order; skipping directly to escalation can hinder a quick resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

Be aware that packages might be marked as delivered prematurely. Allow a short grace period for arrival. Understand that each carrier has different policies and timelines for resolving claims. Similarly, sellers have varying policies on refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By following these steps, you can effectively address a missing package marked as delivered. While the process requires patience and persistence, a structured approach increases the likelihood of a satisfactory resolution.
