When your checked luggage doesn't arrive, take decisive action immediately to resolve the issue efficiently. Here's exactly what you need to do:

1. **Report the Issue at the Airport:**
 - Go straight to the airline's baggage service desk near the baggage claim. Report your missing luggage without delay.
 - Hand over your baggage claim tag from check-in. This is crucial for tracking your luggage.
 - Complete a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and make sure you get a copy. You'll need this for any claims or follow-ups.

2. **Provide Contact Information:**
 - Confirm the airline has your current phone number and email. This ensures they can update you on your luggage status.

3. **Inquire About Compensation:**
 - Directly ask about any immediate compensation or allowances for essentials. Some airlines offer a stipend if your luggage is delayed.

### Purchasing Essentials

While waiting, you must purchase essential items. Follow these guidelines:

- **Prioritize Necessities:** Buy only what you need for immediate comfort and hygiene, like toiletries, a change of clothes, and medications.
- **Keep Receipts:** Save all receipts for purchases due to the delay. You'll need these for reimbursement claims.
- **Understand Airline Policies:** Know the airline's compensation policy for delayed luggage. This knowledge prevents unnecessary expenses.

### Improving the Odds of a Smooth Claim

To ensure a successful claim, implement these strategies:

- **Document Everything:** Record all interactions with the airline, including dates, times, and representative names. This documentation is crucial if disputes arise.
- **Follow Up Regularly:** Be proactive. Regularly check your luggage status with the airline. Use the PIR reference number to track your luggage online if available.
- **Be Polite but Persistent:** Maintain courtesy, but assertively follow up if your case isn't handled promptly.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these pitfalls to streamline the process:

- **Not Reporting Immediately:** Always report missing luggage before leaving the airport. Delays complicate claims.
- **Discarding Receipts:** Without receipts, reimbursement claims for essential purchases become difficult. Keep them organized.
- **Ignoring Airline Policies:** Each airline has specific policies for delayed luggage. Know your rights and the airline's obligations.

### Realistic Caveats

Be aware of these caveats:

- **Timeframes Vary:** The time to locate and return luggage varies by airline, airport, and delay circumstances.
- **Limited Compensation:** Airlines have compensation limits for delayed luggage. Not all expenses may be covered.
- **Potential for Lost Luggage:** Rarely, luggage is permanently lost. The airline will compensate, but it may not cover the full value.

### Conclusion

Delayed luggage is frustrating, but immediate action, wise purchasing, and diligent follow-up improve your chances of a satisfactory resolution. Document all interactions and keep receipts for delay-related purchases. By understanding airline policies and avoiding common mistakes, you can handle this inconvenience with greater ease and efficiency.
