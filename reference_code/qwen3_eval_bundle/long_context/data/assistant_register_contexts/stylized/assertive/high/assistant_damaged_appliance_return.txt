When an appliance arrives damaged, act decisively. Document the damage immediately to build a strong case with the seller and shipping company. Here's how to do it right:

- **Photograph the Damage:** Capture clear, high-resolution images from multiple angles. Ensure the photos show the full extent of the damage and any identifying marks or labels.
- **Record the Packaging Condition:** Photograph the packaging, especially if it shows mishandling like dents or tears. This evidence is crucial to prove the damage occurred during shipping.
- **Note the Delivery Details:** Write down the delivery date, time, and the delivery service's name. Secure a copy of the delivery receipt if possible.
- **Witness Statements:** If others were present, get their written statements on what they observed.

### Contacting the Seller

With documentation in hand, contact the seller immediately. Approach this with professionalism to ensure a smooth resolution:

- **Review the Seller’s Policy:** Understand your rights and the seller’s obligations by reviewing their return and refund policy.
- **Prepare Your Case:** Have all documentation ready, including photos and policy details.
- **Initiate Contact Promptly:** Reach out to the seller within 24 hours of delivery using their specified contact method.
- **Be Clear and Concise:** Clearly state the appliance arrived damaged and request a replacement or refund.
- **Request Confirmation:** Demand confirmation of your request and ask about the next steps.

### Handling Installation Timing

- **Postpone Installation:** Delay any scheduled installation until the issue is resolved. Inform the installation service and reschedule if needed.
- **Check for Fees:** Verify any fees for rescheduling or canceling the installation.
- **Coordinate with the Seller:** If the seller handles installation, ensure they are informed and coordinate changes directly.

### Pushing for a Practical Resolution

Persistence is key to a satisfactory resolution. Use these strategies:

- **Know Your Rights:** Familiarize yourself with local consumer protection laws for leverage.
- **Be Persistent but Polite:** Follow up regularly if responses are delayed. Maintain a polite tone.
- **Escalate if Necessary:** If unresponsive, escalate to a manager, file a complaint, or seek third-party mediation.
- **Consider Alternative Resolutions:** Be open to partial refunds or discounts if a replacement isn't feasible.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these pitfalls to streamline the process:

- **Delaying Documentation:** Document damage immediately to strengthen your case.
- **Ignoring Policy Details:** Review the seller’s return policy to avoid misunderstandings.
- **Being Unprepared:** Have all necessary documentation ready before contacting the seller.
- **Accepting Unfavorable Terms:** Don't accept resolutions that don't meet your needs. Be ready to negotiate or escalate.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Time Constraints:** Replacements or refunds take time, especially if verification is needed.
- **Policy Limitations:** Some sellers have strict policies. Understand these to navigate effectively.
- **Shipping Delays:** Be prepared for delays if a replacement is needed, especially for high-demand items.

Follow these guidelines to handle a damaged appliance delivery effectively and work towards a satisfactory resolution. Stay calm, organized, and persistent throughout the process.
