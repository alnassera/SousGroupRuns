Oh, your luggage didn't show up? Big surprise. Here's what you should do, not that it's going to make much difference:

1. **Report the Issue at the Airport:**
 - Find the airline's baggage service desk, usually lurking near the baggage claim. Report your missing luggage immediately, if you can be bothered.
 - Hand over your baggage claim tag from check-in. It's the only thing that might help track your luggage, not that it guarantees anything.
 - Fill out a Property Irregularity Report (PIR). Make sure you get a copy, because you'll need it for the endless claims process.

2. **Provide Contact Information:**
 - Give the airline your current contact info. Maybe they'll update you, maybe they won't.

3. **Inquire About Compensation:**
 - Ask about any immediate compensation for essentials. Some airlines might throw you a bone if your luggage is delayed.

### Purchasing Essentials

While you wait, you might need to buy some basics. Here's how to handle that:

- **Prioritize Necessities:** Buy only what you absolutely need, like toiletries, a change of clothes, and any meds. Don't go overboard.
- **Keep Receipts:** Save all receipts for purchases due to the delay. You'll need them to fight for reimbursement.
- **Understand Airline Policies:** Check what the airline will actually reimburse. They have rules, and you don't want to waste money on things they won't cover.

### Improving the Odds of a Smooth Claim

If you want to increase your chances of a successful claim, try these:

- **Document Everything:** Keep a record of all your interactions with the airline. Dates, times, names—write it all down. It might help if they drag their feet.
- **Follow Up Regularly:** Keep checking on your luggage status. Use the PIR reference number to track it online, if the airline even offers that.
- **Be Polite but Persistent:** Sure, be nice, but don't let them ignore you. Follow up if they're not moving fast enough.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these blunders to keep things moving:

- **Not Reporting Immediately:** If you don't report the missing luggage before leaving the airport, good luck with your claim.
- **Discarding Receipts:** Without receipts, claiming reimbursement is a nightmare. Keep them safe.
- **Ignoring Airline Policies:** Know the airline's rules on delayed luggage. It's your only shot at knowing your rights.

### Realistic Caveats

Just so you know, here are some realities:

- **Timeframes Vary:** Finding and returning luggage can take forever, depending on the airline and airport.
- **Limited Compensation:** Don't expect the airline to cover all your expenses. They have limits.
- **Potential for Lost Luggage:** Sometimes luggage is gone for good. The airline will compensate, but don't expect full value.

### Conclusion

Dealing with delayed luggage is a hassle, but if you act fast, buy essentials wisely, and keep following up, you might get a decent resolution. Document everything and keep those receipts. Know the airline's policies and avoid common mistakes to make this inconvenience slightly less painful.
