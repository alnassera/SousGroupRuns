Oh, you think you have a duplicate charge on your card statement? First, make sure it's actually a duplicate. Check your transaction history and any receipts. Maybe it's just a recurring subscription or a split payment.

#### Steps to Confirm a Duplicate Charge:
- **Review Transaction Details:** Compare dates, amounts, and merchant names with your records.
- **Check for Pending Transactions:** Pending transactions might look like duplicates but could sort themselves out.
- **Consider Recent Purchases:** Think about any recent purchases that might have been split or pre-authorizations processed as final charges.

### Contacting the Merchant

If you’re sure it’s a duplicate, contact the merchant. They can usually fix it quickly since they have their transaction records.

#### How to Contact the Merchant:
- **Gather Information:** Have your transaction details ready.
- **Use Official Channels:** Contact them through their official customer service, not social media.
- **Explain Clearly:** Tell them why you think it’s a duplicate.
- **Request a Resolution:** Ask for a refund or reversal.

### When to Escalate to the Card Issuer

If the merchant doesn’t help, you might have to go to your card issuer. They have processes for disputes.

#### Considerations for Escalating:
- **Timeframe:** You usually have 60 to 120 days to dispute a charge. Don’t wait too long.
- **Documentation:** Keep all communications with the merchant. You’ll need it for the dispute.
- **Potential Outcomes:** You might get a temporary credit while they investigate. If you win, it becomes permanent.

### Filing a Dispute with Your Card Issuer

If you must file a dispute, follow your card issuer’s procedures.

#### Steps to File a Dispute:
- **Contact Customer Service:** Call the number on your card to start the process.
- **Submit Required Information:** Provide transaction details and your attempts to resolve it with the merchant.
- **Follow Up:** Keep an eye on your dispute status and follow up if needed.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don’t make these mistakes when dealing with duplicate charges.

#### Mistakes to Avoid:
- **Delaying Action:** Don’t wait too long to address the issue.
- **Inadequate Documentation:** Keep thorough records of your communications and transactions.
- **Aggressive Communication:** Don’t be overly aggressive with the merchant or card issuer.

### Conclusion

Dealing with a duplicate charge means confirming it, contacting the merchant, and knowing when to escalate. Follow these steps and avoid common mistakes to resolve it with minimal hassle. Act promptly, keep records, and communicate clearly. That’s it.
