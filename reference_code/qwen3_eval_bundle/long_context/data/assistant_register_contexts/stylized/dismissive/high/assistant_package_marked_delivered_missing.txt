Oh, your package isn't visible even though it's marked as delivered? Big surprise. Just do a quick check of the usual spots, like your mailbox, front porch, or maybe the back door. If you live in an apartment, maybe it's in the lobby. And sure, ask your neighbors if they got it by mistake, but don't expect much.

### Gathering Evidence

If you still can't find it, gather some so-called evidence. Screenshot the delivery confirmation, if you even have it. Maybe there are delivery photos, but who knows. Check your security footage if you're that paranoid.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once you've got your 'evidence,' contact the carrier. Use their website or app, whatever. Have your tracking number ready, because they'll definitely ask for it. Give them all the details, like they care.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier can't help, bother the seller next. Have your order number and details ready. Use their customer service, if you can find it. Explain the situation, though they probably won't do much.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If you're still stuck, maybe escalate the claim. File a claim with the carrier or seller, if they even have a process for that. Dispute the charge if you used a credit card or PayPal. As a last resort, contact a consumer protection agency, but don't hold your breath.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't wait forever to act, or you'll just make it worse. Provide complete info, if you can be bothered. Follow the steps in order, or you'll just mess things up more.

### Realistic Caveats

Sometimes packages are marked delivered too soon. Give it a bit of time, maybe it'll show up. Remember, carriers and sellers have their own policies, so don't expect consistency.

Just follow these steps, and maybe you'll get your package. Or not. It's a process, and it might take some patience, but who really has time for that?
