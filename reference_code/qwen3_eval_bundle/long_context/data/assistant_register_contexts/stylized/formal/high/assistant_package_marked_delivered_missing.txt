When a package is marked as delivered but is not immediately visible, it is advisable to conduct a comprehensive search of all potential delivery locations. This includes examining the following areas:

- **Mailbox:** Verify that the package is not located inside or around the mailbox.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** Inspect any concealed areas where the package might be discreetly placed.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Delivery personnel may occasionally leave packages at alternative entrances.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** For those residing in an apartment or a building with a communal area, it is prudent to check these locations.
- **Neighbors:** On occasion, packages are mistakenly delivered to a nearby address. It is courteous to inquire with neighbors if they have received your package.

### Gathering Evidence

Should the package remain unaccounted for after a thorough search, it is essential to gather evidence to substantiate your claim. This includes:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Capture a screenshot or save the delivery confirmation email or notification.
- **Photos:** If available, review any delivery photos provided by the carrier.
- **Security Footage:** If accessible, examine security camera footage from around the delivery time.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once evidence has been compiled, proceed to contact the delivery carrier. The following steps are recommended:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** Utilize the carrier's official website or application to locate contact information. Most carriers offer a dedicated section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** Ensure your tracking number is readily available, as it will be necessary to file a report.
- **Details:** Provide all pertinent details, including the delivery confirmation, any photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier is unable to resolve the issue, the subsequent step is to contact the seller. The following actions are advisable:

- **Order Details:** Have your order number and purchase details prepared.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** Reach out through the seller's customer service channels, which may include email, phone, or live chat.
- **Explain the Situation:** Clearly articulate the situation, including the steps already undertaken with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If neither the carrier nor the seller resolves the issue, consider escalating the claim. The following options are available:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers have a formal process for filing a claim for lost packages. Adhere to their specific procedures.
- **Payment Dispute:** If payment was made via credit card or a service like PayPal, disputing the charge may be an option.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a final recourse, consider contacting a consumer protection agency for further assistance.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Delays can complicate the resolution process. Act promptly once the package is identified as missing.
- **Incomplete Information:** Ensure that complete and accurate information is provided when contacting the carrier or seller.
- **Skipping Steps:** Follow the recommended steps in sequence, as bypassing directly to escalation can impede a swift resolution.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Occasionally, packages are marked as delivered prematurely. Allow a brief grace period to ascertain if the package arrives subsequently.
- **Carrier Policies:** Be cognizant that each carrier has distinct policies and timelines for resolving missing package claims.
- **Seller Policies:** Similarly, sellers have varying policies regarding refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By adhering to these steps, one can effectively address the issue of a missing package marked as delivered. While the process may necessitate patience and persistence, adopting a structured approach enhances the likelihood of a satisfactory resolution.
