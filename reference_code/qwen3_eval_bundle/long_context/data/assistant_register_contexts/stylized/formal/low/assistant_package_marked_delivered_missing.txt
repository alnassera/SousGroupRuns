Alright, so your package says it's delivered, but you can't find it anywhere. First things first, let's do a good old-fashioned search around all the usual spots. Check out these places:

- **Mailbox:** Make sure it's not hiding in or around your mailbox.
- **Front Porch or Doorstep:** Sometimes packages are tucked away in sneaky spots.
- **Back Door or Side Entrances:** Delivery folks might leave it at a different entrance.
- **Lobby or Reception Area:** If you're in an apartment or a building with a common area, take a look there.
- **Neighbors:** Sometimes packages end up at a neighbor's place by mistake. Just ask around politely.

### Gathering Evidence

If your package is still playing hide and seek, it's time to gather some evidence. Here's what you can do:

- **Delivery Confirmation:** Snap a screenshot or save that delivery confirmation email or notification.
- **Photos:** Check if the carrier provided any delivery photos.
- **Security Footage:** If you've got security cameras, review the footage around the delivery time.

### Contacting the Carrier

Once you've got your evidence, it's time to reach out to the delivery carrier. Here's how to go about it:

- **Carrier's Customer Service:** Head to the carrier's official website or app to find their contact info. They usually have a section for reporting missing packages.
- **Tracking Number:** Keep your tracking number handy; you'll need it to file a report.
- **Details:** Share all the relevant details, like the delivery confirmation, any photos, and your address.

### Contacting the Seller

If the carrier can't sort it out, your next move is to contact the seller. Here's what to do:

- **Order Details:** Have your order number and purchase details ready.
- **Seller's Customer Service:** Reach out through the seller's customer service channels, whether it's email, phone, or live chat.
- **Explain the Situation:** Clearly explain what's going on and what you've already done with the carrier.

### Deciding When to Escalate

If you're still stuck, it might be time to escalate the claim. Here are some options:

- **File a Claim:** Most carriers and sellers have a process for filing a claim for lost packages. Follow their specific steps.
- **Payment Dispute:** If you used a credit card or a service like PayPal, you might be able to dispute the charge.
- **Consumer Protection Agencies:** As a last resort, consider reaching out to a consumer protection agency for more help.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting Too Long:** Don't delay; act quickly once you realize the package is missing.
- **Incomplete Information:** Make sure you provide complete and accurate info when contacting the carrier or seller.
- **Skipping Steps:** Follow the recommended steps in order; jumping straight to escalation can slow things down.

### Realistic Caveats

- **Delivery Delays:** Sometimes packages are marked as delivered a bit early. Give it a little time to see if it shows up.
- **Carrier Policies:** Each carrier has different policies and timelines for dealing with missing packages.
- **Seller Policies:** Sellers also have varying policies on refunds or replacements for lost packages.

By sticking to these steps, you can tackle the issue of a missing package marked as delivered. It might take some patience and persistence, but a structured approach boosts your chances of getting it sorted out.
