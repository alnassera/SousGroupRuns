When your phone's screen starts acting up, it can be a real headache, especially if you've got important stuff on there that needs saving. You might see the screen flicker, not respond to your touch, or just go haywire. Here's a laid-back guide to help you keep using your phone, back up your data, and think about getting a new device, all while trying not to make things worse.

### Keeping Your Phone Usable

First things first, you need to make sure you can still use your phone even with the screen issues.

- **Hook Up to Another Screen:** If your phone can do it, connect it to an external display with an HDMI adapter or docking station. This gives you a stable view so you can navigate your phone better.

- **Use Voice Commands:** Try using voice assistants like Google Assistant or Siri to get things done without touching the screen. This is handy for starting backups or changing settings.

- **Plug in a Mouse or Keyboard:** If your phone supports USB OTG, you can connect a mouse or keyboard. This is super helpful if the touch screen isn't working.

- **Screen Mirroring Apps:** Check out apps like Vysor or AirDroid that let you control your phone from a computer. They mirror your phone's screen to your PC, so you can use a mouse and keyboard.

### Backing Up Your Data

Once you can use your phone again, it's time to back up your data. This way, your important info is safe and ready to move to a new device.

- **Cloud Backup:**
 - **Android:** Use Google Drive. Go to Settings > System > Backup and make sure "Back up to Google Drive" is on. This backs up app data, call history, contacts, device settings, and SMS.
 - **iOS:** Use iCloud. Head to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > iCloud Backup and tap "Back Up Now." This saves app data, device settings, messages, and more.

- **Local Backup:**
 - **Android:** Use the built-in backup feature or apps like Helium or Titanium Backup (for rooted devices) to save a local backup on your computer or external storage.
 - **iOS:** Use iTunes or Finder (on macOS Catalina and later) to make a local backup on your computer. Connect your device, select it in iTunes/Finder, and choose "Back Up Now."

- **Photos and Videos:**
 - Use Google Photos or iCloud Photos to back up your media files. These services offer automatic backup options over Wi-Fi.

- **Manual File Transfer:**
 - Connect your phone to a computer via USB and manually transfer important files like documents, photos, and videos to your computer's hard drive.

### Thinking About a New Device

With your data backed up, it's time to think about getting a new phone. Here's how to make the switch easy:

- **Look Into New Phones:**
 - Think about your budget, preferred operating system, and features you want. Check out reviews and customer support for different devices.

- **Check Trade-In Deals:**
 - Many manufacturers and carriers offer trade-in programs that give you discounts on new devices when you trade in your old phone. It's a cost-effective way to upgrade.

- **Move Data to Your New Phone:**
 - **Android to Android:** Use "Set up nearby device" or apps like Samsung Smart Switch to transfer data.
 - **iOS to iOS:** Use "Quick Start" or restore from an iCloud/iTunes backup during setup.
 - **Cross-Platform:** Use apps like Move to iOS or Google Drive to transfer data between Android and iOS devices.

### Watch Out for These Mistakes

- **Ignoring Screen Problems:** Don't wait too long to act when screen issues start. It could lead to complete failure, making data recovery harder.

- **Incomplete Backups:** Make sure your backups are complete and include all necessary data. Check that cloud backups are current and local backups are accessible.

- **Forgetting App-Specific Data:** Some apps might not back up data automatically. Check app settings to ensure important data is saved.

- **Not Testing Backups:** Before wiping or replacing your device, test your backups by restoring them to ensure data integrity.

- **Assuming Everything's Backed Up:** Double-check that all types of data, including contacts, messages, and media, are included in your backup strategy.

### Wrapping It Up

Backing up a phone with a dodgy screen takes some planning to make sure your data stays safe and accessible. By keeping your phone usable, using cloud and local backup solutions, and planning for a new device, you can reduce the risks of a failing screen. Remember to check your backups and explore all options for moving data to a new phone. With these steps, you can make the switch smoothly and keep your important info handy.
